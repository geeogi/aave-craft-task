Aave V3 Ethereum is chosen because it is the primary target for user
acquisition for Aave V4 and similarly positioned. Also, it is the largest DeFi
lending market and understanding its userbase is important. Short term
competitor is Plasma/Mantle but focussing on that market as an aquisition
target feels self-competitive. The goal is to identify the type of users who
have grown Aave V3 so we can attract more of them.

Metrics are skewed towards capital distribution as opposed to user counts,
because market size is the leading KPI.

# User actions

We find the USD value of user actions using *today's* pricing. This helps us
standardise actions through time and across markets and prevent being skewed
by price movements.

In other words, the actions show us the volume of user activity relative to
current pricing, not relative to any historical pricing. This means that
supplying 1 ETH today is counted as similar to supplying 1 ETH two years ago.

USD is not historically accruate, but is added to aid comparison through time.

Use actions are leaky, because users can change their position through
transfers, not captured here.

Activity retention - as opposed to balance retention - is useful because many
users do not hold balances through time, they use Aave when they need it.

Users split activity across wallets so a simple MAU chart may be more
reflective.

# V3 User Balances

V3 is not scaling balances correctly. It shows the aToken amounts at time of
transfer and does not account interest that has accrued since transfer.

Positions in V3 can be transferred between addresses by way of an
aToken/vToken transfer. This means that supply/withdraw/borrow/repay events do
not tell the whole story of balances.

Computing the current balances of all V3 users requires the full list of
aToken addresses. For simplicity I have worked with a focussed list of the top
reserves.

To get better precision around V3 balances we use the liquidityIndex weekly to
scale by interest accrued.

V3 balances are sanity checked using Etherscan to view aToken balances.

# Retention

I choose $100 as a threshold for retention because lower than that the
protocol is not useful.

For dollar retention we use prices-at-cohort-week to reduce skew from market
prices. This is not perfect because many users are price sensitive and will
adjust positions because of market pricing.
